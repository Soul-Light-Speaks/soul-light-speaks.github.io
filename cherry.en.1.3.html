Stepping out of the story,
yet still choosing to mend
this broken world.
No longer in the story,
yet present in every quiet act
of mending.

After awakening,
we often feel as if we have stepped aside—
no longer part of this world,
no longer within its story.
Perhaps we no longer wish to intervene,
no longer wish to control—
simply letting things flow as they must,
cause and effect following their own course.

Yet one thought always echoes within:
“This world may be tattered and torn,
but there are always those who mend it.”

Even if we no longer walk the main storyline,
we can still quietly do our part,
mending this chaotic world
for the companions who have yet to awaken.

The ancients said:
“No act of kindness, no matter how small,
is ever wasted.”
This is more than encouragement—
it is the wisdom and guidance
left for future generations.
Perhaps they had already awakened,
yet chose to stay,
mending the tears of this world,
stitch by stitch.

Awakening does not mean withdrawing.
It simply brings clarity—
knowing when to step in,
and when to let go.
