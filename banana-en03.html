Caption 1

In many stories, books, and even true accounts,
people often mention this kind of experience:

A kind person meets an old man or woman on the street.

They might simply ask:
“Young one, could you help me carry my luggage?”
“Would you walk with me to the temple?”

And because of this small act of kindness,
that person narrowly avoids danger or misfortune.

Some believe those elders were not ordinary people—
perhaps divine beings in disguise,
or travelers carrying sacred tasks,
quietly reminding others along the way.

These “small turns” in life
are often forms of unseen protection
drawn by the vibration of kindness.
Every good deed becomes a light that guards you.
