Once, you tried to save someone—
but could not.
That helplessness
became hope for those who came after.
A gift from family,
a mission for a lifetime.

Many who enter medicine later
aren’t chasing fame.

At first, they only wanted to save one person—
to undo the helplessness they felt back then.

They wanted to save a loved one lying in a hospital bed,
to turn back time.

But time does not wait.
Some departures come too soon—
not failures,
but pain frozen in that generation,
becoming the force that drives a life.

The loss of family
is not meant to drown you in sorrow.
It is a handover of a task.

Their presence
lets you carry wounds forward.
And your effort
will spare others some tears.

You keep learning, improving, innovating.
And slowly, the same tragedies stop repeating.

Perhaps family appeared
to end that suffering,
to stop it in this generation.

And you remain,
to transform that helplessness
into ability.

So those who come after
need not bear the same pain.

So if it was too late,
do not see it as regret.

It was their last faith in you,
entrusting you with
a mission that spans a lifetime.
